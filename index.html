The Experience

Coffee cupping is the professional method for tasting and evaluating coffee — and on March 14th, you're the judges.

How It Works

You'll taste 8 coffees side by side, each brewed the same simple way: freshly ground coffee steeped directly in hot water. No filters, no gadgets — just coffee, water, and a spoon. This strips away all variables and lets the coffee speak for itself.

How to Taste

Smell first — both the dry grounds and after water is added. Slurp loudly — seriously, the louder the better. Slurping aerates the coffee across your whole palate. Think about what fruits, flavors, or textures come to mind. There are no wrong answers.

The Flavor Wheel

Coffee can taste like blueberries, jasmine, brown sugar, red wine, peaches, dark chocolate, or even tomato soup. If you taste it, it's real. Your palate is valid. Trust what you experience.

No Experience Needed

You don't need to be a coffee expert — just bring curiosity and an open mind. We'll walk you through every step. You'll get a tasting card to jot notes as you go. The only rule: have fun.

Cupping is how coffee professionals taste and score coffees around the world. Every coffee is brewed the exact same way — same dose, same water, same time — so the only variable is the coffee itself. Here's a quick look at what it's all about, followed by exactly what we'll do together.

Round 1 — The Guided Tasting

We'll walk through the first 2 coffees together, step by step. Just follow along — no experience needed.

01

Look at the Table

You'll see rows of identical cups, each filled with coarsely ground coffee. Every cup has the same amount, ground the same way. There's a reason for this — when everything else is equal, the only thing you're comparing is the coffee itself. You'll also have a spoon and a rinse cup.

02

Smell the Dry Grounds

Before any water is added, lean in and smell each cup. This is the dry fragrance — you're catching the most volatile aromatics before they disappear. Does it smell like chocolate? Fruit? Toast? Flowers? There's no wrong answer. Just notice what comes to mind and jot it on your tasting card.

03

Watch the Pour

Hot water (about 200°F) is poured directly onto the grounds. You'll see the coffee bloom and a thick crust of grounds form on the surface, trapping all those aromatics underneath like a lid. Now we wait four minutes. Use this time to observe — the crust will shift and bubble slightly as gases release.

04

Break the Crust

This is the best moment. Take your spoon and push through the floating crust with three gentle strokes. Get your nose right over the cup as you do this — a burst of aroma releases that's often completely different from the dry fragrance. This is the wet aroma. After breaking, skim off the remaining grounds floating on top.

05

Slurp — Loudly

Scoop a spoonful and slurp it as loudly as you can. Yes, really. This isn't polite tea sipping — slurping sprays the coffee across your entire palate and sends aromatics up through your nasal passage. Professionals sound absolutely ridiculous doing this. You will too. That's the point. Rinse your spoon between cups.

06

Notice What You Notice

As the coffee hits your palate, pay attention to a few things: Is it bright and zingy, or smooth and mellow? That's acidity. Does it feel light like tea or thick like cream? That's body. What flavors come to mind? Fruit, chocolate, nuts, flowers — anything goes. And what happens after you swallow? Does the flavor linger or vanish? Write it all down. Trust your palate.

07

Go Back as It Cools

Here's a secret: coffee changes dramatically as it cools. A cup that seems quiet when hot can open up into something incredible at room temperature. Go back to each cup every few minutes. The best coffees keep revealing new layers. This is why we don't rush.

Round 2 — The Blind Tasting

Now you know the ritual. For the next 4 coffees, you're on your own.

★

You've Got This

Four new cups are set up — but this time, you won't know what they are. No names, no origins, no hints. Smell, break, slurp, and evaluate at your own pace. Use your tasting card to score each one. Compare cups. Go back to favorites. Discuss with the people around you. This is where your own palate takes the lead.

★

Vote on Your Favorites

Once everyone has tasted all four, you'll vote on your top picks. Which one stood out? Which surprised you? Which would you want to drink every morning? Then comes the best part — the big reveal. We'll uncover the name, origin, processing method, and price of each coffee. Prepare to be surprised.

Words You Might Hear

A quick cheat sheet so nothing catches you off guard.

Acidity

Not sour — think bright, sparkly, lively. Like the zing of a green apple or citrus. It's a good thing in coffee.

Body

How the coffee feels in your mouth. Light and tea-like? Syrupy and heavy? It's all about texture, and there's no wrong answer.

Natural Process

The coffee cherry dries around the bean before removal, infusing it with fruity, wine-like flavors. Think blueberry, strawberry, tropical fruit.

Washed Process

The fruit is removed before drying, producing a cleaner, brighter cup that lets the bean's terroir shine through.

Co-Ferment

Coffee fermented alongside fruits or other ingredients. The coffee absorbs those flavors during fermentation — it's intentional, not artificial.

Anaerobic

Fermented in a sealed, oxygen-free environment. Creates funky, complex, sometimes boozy flavors you won't find in conventional coffee.

The Grind & Dose

Every cup is ground coarse — like rough sand — and dosed at exactly 8.3g per 150ml of water. Same ratio, same grind, every cup. The only variable is the coffee.

The Water

Coffee is 98% water. So yeah — the water matters.

Why We Built the Water From Scratch

Tap water contains minerals that change how coffee extracts and tastes — too many and the coffee tastes flat, too few and it tastes hollow and sharp. For today's cupping, we started with distilled water (zero minerals) and added back exactly what coffee needs to taste its best.
